## 1. The load-bearing finding (dm-0115)

The P10 execution plan assumed "every mechanism is built; P10 is just wiring +
data." A 144-level zero-assumption sweep through the real pipeline **disproved
that premise** and reshaped the phase:

- Under `DEFAULT_PROFILE`, every generated gap-corridor **passes info-density but
  fails the emotional gate** (`surprise` scored **0.0 on all 144 levels** vs the
  threshold 95 — `surprise = 32 × deaths-before-a-clear`, and the deterministic
  archetype agents almost always either clean-clear or time out, so
  plan-invalidation is never registered) **and streamability**.
- The four **approach-modifying** mechanics (`movingPlatform`, `collapsingFloor`,
  `iceSurface`, `conveyor`) **softlock or make unsolvable ~100%** of one-jump
  corridors (a spent jump plus a modified approach traps the player). Seven
  mechanic configs are clean: the pit `spike`, `laser`, `movingHazard`, `spring`,
  `gravityZone`, `pressurePlate`+`door`, `proximityZone`.

The user chose the **"machinery + honest close"** path: build and test all
content machinery, calibrate the profile via CDRE to the achievable distribution,
generate the real campaign, flip only genuinely-satisfied REQs to VERIFIED, and
keep the generator-limited REQs IN_PROGRESS with an explicit disclosure. **No gate
pass is faked; VERIFIED P7 is not reopened.** Enriching the generator with
subversion templates (to earn genuine surprise) is a scoped P7+ follow-on, not
this phase.

## 4. Honest capability disclosure

What P10 genuinely delivers, and what it does not:

- **Delivered:** the full content authoring/validation layer; a real campaign of 36
  one-jump levels manufactured by GDOS-driven overgenerate-and-curate (REQ-050),
  every one solvable, jump-necessary (REQ-012), dual-path (REQ-100), intent-verified
  (REQ-091), integrated (REQ-090 phase 8), and curriculum-valid (REQ-142); the seven-step
  chapter framework (REQ-083); per-level non-IAP rewarded-skip data (REQ-174 share).
- **Generator-limited (kept IN_PROGRESS):**
  - **REQ-005** — "Level-Devil-style subversion." The gap-corridor produces near-zero
    measured surprise; genuine betrayal needs new generator templates (trapdoors,
    fake floors, delayed/triggered hazards). Not delivered.
  - **REQ-084** — difficulty distribution. The generator cannot span the five tiers;
    measured distribution is bimodal (easy/hard). Enforcement built, content cannot pass.
  - **REQ-015** — the arc is *structurally* complete and enforced, but the emotional
    *delivery* of the Surprise/Betrayal phase is weak for the same reason as REQ-005;
    kept IN_PROGRESS for the P11 final-compliance audit.
  - **REQ-174 / REQ-016 / REQ-002** — data/content shares delivered; the runtime
    `rewardedBreak` wiring, fairness-under-load, and ship-surface remainders are P11.

The calibration (`content-calibration-v1`, dm-0116) lowered only gate **thresholds**
(never estimator coefficients) to the generator's achievable band, via the P5 CDRE
channel, ledgered — distribution-level, **not** a per-level override to ship a
specific weak level. A future richer generator should raise the thresholds back
toward `DEFAULT_PROFILE` and re-verify.
